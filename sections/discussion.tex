\section{Discussion}

\subsection{Generalizability}
One concern might lie in the generalizability of DPSL. After all, quite a few assumptions were made during the problem formulation that allowed the optimal value functions to be derived using dynamic programming. Who is to say these results generalize to real trading scenarios, or other markets?

The strongest assumptions that were made are that trade volume does not influence the price at which current and future trades are executed (zero market impact), and that we assume only reward functions are used that are independent of equity.
